“He was born, he worked, and he died.” So goes Martin Heidegger’s famous
biographical remark on Aristotle,1

Vol. 11, No. 1 (June 2014), 115–129 
On a Man Who Died from Reading Too Much
Heidegger, or Richard Rorty as a Reader
Wojciech Małecki
115
 
Martin Heidegger once said, “What was Aristotle’s life? Well, the answer lies in a single sentence: He was born, he thought, he died. And all the rest is pure anecdote.”[1]

[1] Excerpts from Derrida’s remarks can be seen in the film Derrida by Kirby Dick and Amy Ziering Kofman (DVD available from Blaq Out editions, 2007).
